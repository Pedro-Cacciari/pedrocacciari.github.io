Welcome to the Geomechanics & Rock Mass Modeling (GRMM) research group

Polytechnique Montréal

Our group conducts research and consulting projects in geomechanics, with a focus on experimental rock mechanics and discontinuous rock mass modeling applied to rock excavation stability and fluid flow in fractured media.

Our main areas of expertise include:

  • Laboratory testing in rock mechanics
  • Mechanical behavior of anisotropic rocks
  • Strength of geological structures
  • Mapping and analysis of discontinuities using 3D imaging
  • Fractured rock mass modeling using discrete fracture networks (DFN)
  • Development of new DFN modeling tools and implementation of custom numerical codes
  • Stability analysis of rock excavations using deterministic and probabilistic methods

Research highlights

  • DFN modeling and anisotropic rock mass behavior – Using DFN-DEM to assess the mechanical behavior of anisotropic rock masses and enhance the stability analysis of rock excavations.
  • DFN modeling and fluid flow – Using DFN modeling to improve water flow analysis in fractured rock masses. We develop new DFN-based tools to assess rock mass permeability, evaluate infiltration potential in tunnels, and design optimized pipe network models tailored to specific engineering challenges.
  • Laboratory testing using 3D-printed rock masses – We develop methods to print DFNs within synthetic materials. These physical models are used to validate numerical and analytical approaches and to improve water flow solutions applied in rock engineering
